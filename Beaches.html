OGTOC ISLAND

Ogtoc is the perfect destination for a nature walk because it has fine sand, crystal clear water, and breathtaking scenery. This island has a total land area of 93 hectares and is situated in Sitio Ogtoc, Barangay Buenasuerte. Although it is officially known as Saboon Island, the locals simply refer to it as "Ogtoc," and outdoor enthusiasts refer to it as "Paradise Island." It is still unknown where the name "ogtoc" originated.

The island is ideal for nature-based, sun-and-beach, marine and water-sports tourism. The main activities on the island are swimming and sunbathing, but the marine protected areas are ideal for those who enjoy sea adventures such as snorkeling, scuba diving, island hopping, sailing/boating, kayaking, canoeing, and marine life observation. Other recreational activities that the island can provide for visitors include beach and wilderness camping, hiking, team building, family or peer bonding, trainings, and many more.

Having the best time of your life on the island will not be complete unless you witness this wonderful moment that separates night and day. Arrive on the island as early as possible to catch the rising sun as it crosses the eastern mountain range and descends to the western horizon. If you're lucky, the "nabarko" sand bar, as the locals call it, will emerge from the water during low tide.

A man-made watchtower built on the top of Pinong Dahon islet provides a 360-degree view of the entire island and the Ragay gulf.

Except during bad weather, this island can be visited at any time of year. However, the best time to visit will be during the summer, from March to May. The local electric cooperative provides power to the island. However, due to its isolation from the mainland, other utilities and amenities such as water supply, food, and lodging houses have yet to be installed. Some parts of the island may experience poor communication network signal.

Barangay San Rafael is the closest point of access to the island. However, it is also accessible from Brgy. Apad-aparan's Sitio Apad-aparan.of Brgy. Buenasuerte and Sitio Mamalao of Brgy. Binahan Proper, which are also nearby. The only way to get to the island is by sea, using a motorboat/banca for a rental fee of 1,000, back and forth, for a maximum of 10-15 people. The island is only a 20-minute boat ride away. There's a lot more to Ogtoc. All you need to do is visit the island to prove it for yourself.

BAGOTAYOK BEACH

Bagotayoc Beach is distinguished by its white sand, crystal clear water, and cool breeze.

Swimming and boating are among the activities available to visitors. The beach's tranquility, splashing waves, whirling green leaves, and chirping birds were all you could hear and see, making it ideal for reflection and meditation.

Bagotayok Beach is the pride of Barangay Lohong and measures approximately 15 hectares in width. Long before the formal settlers arrived, the term "bagotayok" was coined.

The months of February through May are ideal for beach visits. However, the beach can be visited at any time of year as long as the weather is pleasant.

The beach can be reached by land travel for about 40-45 minutes via Andaya Highway from the town proper. Privately owned cars, habal-habal, and tricycles can all be used as modes of transportation. of transportation. A tricycle rental to the area will cost between 70 and 80 pesos.

However, it will take 300 meters walk for about 10 minutes to reach the entertaining beach.

Cottage owners charge a small entrance fee, cottage fees ranging from P300 to P500, and a tent rental fee of P500 for the entire day.
